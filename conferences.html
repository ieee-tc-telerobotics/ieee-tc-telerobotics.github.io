Conferences/Workshops

Robotics For Combating COVID-19:

COVID should serve as a catalyst to reconsider, at a fundamental level, the paradigms of healthcare delivery and how more extensive, targeted, and deliberate use of technology can reduce the loss of human lives and can alleviate the burden on the healthcare staff.

Supported Workshops:

  • Workshop entitled “User-centered methods in human-robot interaction,” IROS 2018, organized by Gionata Salvietti, Philipp Beckerle, and Matteo Bianchi.
  • Workshop “Teleoperation of Dynamic Legged Robots in Real Scenarios” submitted to the 2021 ICRA conference by Kourosh Darvish, Joao Ramos, Rafael Cisneros, Luigi Penco, Eiichi Yoshida, Serena Ivaldi, and Daniele Pucci.
  • Workshop “Impact of COVID-19 on Medical Robotics and Wearables Research: Learning from the Past and Strategizing for the Future” submitted to the 2021 ICRA conference by Prof. Mahdi Tavakoli, Prof. S. Farokh Atashzar, Prof. Kaspar Althoefer, and Prof. Paolo Fiorini.
  • Workshop “From gears to direct drive: Recent trends and opportunities in actuation” submitted to the 2021 IROS conference by Navvab Kashiri, Tom Verstraten, Raphaël Guy Furnémont, Yasutaka Fujimoto, Stefano Stramigioli, Nikos Tsagarakis and Wesley Roozing.
